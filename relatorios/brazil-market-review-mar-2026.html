4. Structural Cycles & Regime Assessment

Where Are We in Each Cycle?

CyclePositionImplication
Debt SupercycleLATE STAGEBrazil gross debt approaching 95% GDP. Fiscal dominance trap: high rates needed to attract financing, which chokes growth, which worsens ratio. No credible consolidation path in election year.
Commodity SupercycleEARLY-MIDDecade of capex underinvestment + new AI/electrification demand. Copper, uranium, gold structurally undersupplied through 2030. Positive for Brazil as commodity exporter.
Interest Rate RegimeHIGHER-FOR-LONGERConfirmed structural shift. US 10Y at 4.40%. Fed neutral rate now ~3%. Brazil Selic settles at 10-11% even in benign case. Growth/duration assets permanently repriced.
AI / ProductivityCAPEX NOW, PRODUCTIVITY 2030s$667B tech capex in 2026 but 1% adoption maturity. Goldman: "no meaningful economy-wide productivity impact yet." Macro GDP impact projected 1.5% by 2035.
DeglobalizationMODERATE TAILWINDIDB: nearshoring could add ~$20B/yr for LatAm. Brazil benefits in agri, energy, mining. Manufacturing goes to Mexico.
DemographicsSLOW HEADWINDTFR 1.62 (below replacement). Median age 35.3 → 44.9 by 2050. Labor force growth slowing. 10-20 year drag on growth and pension systems.

Regime Stress Test (Dalio Quadrants)

Q1: Overheating (Rising Growth + Rising Inflation)

Probability: 25%
  • Winners: LFTs, NTN-Bs (inflation linkage), commodities (VALE3, PETR4), Gold, US floating rate
  • Losers: Prefixado, AGG, FIIs, growth stocks
  • Key risk: Hormuz keeping oil elevated while fiscal expansion in election year adds demand pressure

Q2: Goldilocks (Rising Growth + Falling Inflation)

Probability: 15% — Least likely given structural forces
  • Winners: Everything rallies — NTN-Bs, equities, FIIs, international
  • Losers: LFTs underperform as Selic falls
  • Trigger: Hormuz resolved + AI productivity surprise + China stimulus

Q3: Stagflation (Falling Growth + Rising Inflation)

Probability: 30% — HIGHEST RISK scenario
  • Winners: Gold, LFTs (if BCB hikes), NTN-Bs (inflation linkage), USD assets
  • Losers: Prefixado destroyed, BR equities, FIIs, corporate credit, growth ETFs
  • Trigger: Fiscal dominance + sustained oil shock + election spending

Q4: Deflation / Recession (Falling Growth + Falling Inflation)

Probability: 30%
  • Winners: Prefixado (star performers — rates collapse), NTN-Bs, AGG, defensive dividends
  • Losers: Commodities (VALE3, PETR4), BR equities broadly, corporate credit, FIIs
  • Trigger: China hard landing, global credit event, aggressive BCB tightening tips Brazil into recession

8. Sell / Avoid Signals

Prefixado (LTN) Long Duration UNDERWEIGHT

Fixed-rate bondsStagflation vulnerable

Fixed-rate bonds are destroyed in a stagflation scenario (30% probability). The Hormuz oil shock creates upside inflation risk that could stall COPOM cuts — exactly the scenario that kills prefixado. Only appropriate for those with high conviction on the goldilocks outcome (15% probability). Reduce duration, keep only short-dated positions.

Private Credit at Compressed Spreads CAUTION

Spreads: historical lowsDefaults: record 4.2%81.3M inadimplentes

The most dangerous mismatch in Brazilian fixed income right now. Credit spreads on incentivized debentures are at historical lows per Sparta, while system-wide defaults hit 4.2% (highest since series began in 2011). Free-market lending default at 5.5%, projected to reach 7-8% by mid-2026. Stick to AAA infrastructure debentures only. Avoid new issuances at current spreads.

US Floating Rate (USFR) — Overweight REDUCE

Yield ~5.36%Fed heading to 3%

Floating rate yield compresses with each Fed cut. Fed at 3.50-3.75% heading toward 3.00% by end-2027. For investors overweight USFR, redirect to AGG (captures price appreciation as rates fall) or NTN-B IPCA+ 2035 which offers dramatically higher real yield (IPCA+7.3% vs ~5.36% nominal).

Key Structural Insight

Most Brazilian portfolios are implicitly positioned for "goldilocks" (falling rates + stable growth) — which is the least likely scenario at 15% probability. The highest-probability adverse scenario is stagflation (30%): fiscal dominance + sustained oil shock + election year spending. A portfolio without gold, without explicit inflation hedges, and overweight in prefixado and growth assets is structurally unprepared for this outcome. The goal is not to predict which regime comes next, but to ensure the portfolio survives any regime.
